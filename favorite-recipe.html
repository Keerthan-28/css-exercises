Masala Dosa

A popular South Indian savory crepe filled with spiced potato — crispy on the outside, soft inside.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dosa batter (rice + urad dal batter)
  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 medium onion, finely sliced
  • 1–2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds, 1 tsp urad dal, few curry leaves
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder, salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil/ghee for cooking

Instructions

  1. Prepare the potato masala: heat oil, add mustard seeds, urad dal and curry leaves. Sauté onions and green chilies until translucent.
  2. Add turmeric and mashed potatoes, mix well, season with salt and cook for 2–3 minutes. Keep aside.
  3. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron tawa. Pour a ladle of dosa batter and spread thin in a circular motion to form a crepe.
  4. Drizzle a little oil/ghee around the edges and cook until the underside is golden and crisp.
  5. Place a portion of potato masala on one side, fold the dosa, and serve hot with chutney and sambar.

A brief history / interesting fact

Masala dosa is believed to have originated in the southern Indian state of Karnataka and gained popularity across India during the 20th century. It evolved from the plain dosa (a fermented rice and lentil crepe) by adding a spiced potato filling. Today it is a beloved breakfast and street-food item — celebrated for its contrast of textures and savory spices.

Tip: For extra crispiness, ferment dosa batter overnight and spread batter thinly on a very hot griddle.
